Chapter I · A first glance
some memories arrive before we understand them

A Strange Face.
A Familiar Feeling.

One evening, at Ritesh Sir's home, I saw a strange face.

My heart skipped a little.

Something inside me felt different—because somehow, everything had fallen into place.

The next few days, I came to tuition not just to study.

I came simply to be there. To feel that strange shift inside me. To catch a glimpse of you.

And then, one day… you stopped coming.

✦ ✧

And just like that, it disappeared.

As if nothing had ever happened.

Chapter II · A familiar summer

Summer, 2014
Class 9

One scorching afternoon, I joined a new tuition class.

Durga Sir's tuition.

Arpit and Rutu had asked me to join.

I had no clue who the teacher was, who studied there, or what awaited me.

I entered the room, drenched in sweat.

And then my heart skipped again.

The same eyes. The same innocence. The same face that had quietly lingered in my memory.

Time stopped for a moment.
Everything froze.
There you were.

The next few days, I arrived with a little more excitement.

Every day, I studied a little better—maybe because I wanted to impress you, or maybe because simply being in the same room felt like enough.

Chapter III · The best summer

The summer that stayed.

Then you came to Ritesh Sir's tuition too.

It all felt the same again—the same excitement, the same energy.

You started appearing in my dreams.

By then, I think I knew what I wanted.

Honestly, it was one of the best summers I had ever had.

♡

Then came the summer break.

and I missed you from far away.

Small chapters · The holidays

Dussehra Vacation

You came back. And so did the same version of me. Excited. Nervous. Happy for no reason.

Christmas Vacation

By then, I was always excited for holidays—not because of the holidays themselves, but because I knew there was a chance I might see you.

Chapter IV · Ganesh Puja

September 17, 2015

An opportunity quietly knocked at my door.

We were in Class 10 by then.

We had never even chatted—not once.

But that day, I had been assigned to invite guests to school.

And I wondered… what if I invited your dad?

tiny mission

Replace the principal's name. Add one very important name. Keep hoping.

So I replaced the principal's name with your dad's.

And I kept hoping. Kept praying.

That maybe… you would show up.

And you did.

You really did show up.

That was the moment I knew: if it was ever going to be anyone, it would be you.

Chapter VII · A night bus, a little courage

March 3, 2016

I was on a bus, on my way back from Bhubaneswar.

I gathered every bit of courage I had.

And then I told you how I felt.

You rejected me.

But it was okay.

I did not give up.

You chose friendship.

And I chose to stay.

📱

We chatted. We talked. Slowly, we became part of each other's days.

And when I went to hostel, our chats became calls.

I waited every evening…

Just to hear your voice. Just to talk to you. Just to feel close to you.
